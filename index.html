A Current State of Affairs

Today, 'If you don't have an online presence, you simply do not exist,' holds truer today more-so than it ever has before.

Don't stop there.

What if I were to tell you that more than half of U.S. consumers now own a cellphone capable of browsing the internet and those cellphones are now used to plan trips, find businesses, find products and services. In 2013, there were over 1.2 billion mobile users worldwide. The U.S. alone, accounted for 25% of mobile-only traffic. The importance of being mobile first is dire.

Let us also not forget about the growing world of tablet PCs and desktop PCs. In December 2013, the two of them accounted for over 50% of all web traffic. Both platforms are here to stay quite some time.

3 months, a slave.

April 5th, 2013

It's been a long very and positive three months. I've just completed the Front-End Web Development program at Thinkful. My experience cannot be measured in simple words. One might say that ol' Dave has come quite a long way. Others might say, well, they'd probably agree too. Ha! Ok, so not really a slave. Especially when doing something you enjoy.

Now, the next three months are what's going to be interesting. My mentor, Justin, gave me a quest to embark on that will take some time to learn. Node.js, Angular.js, PHP, mongoDB, to name a few things that pique my interest as of right now. To be honest, it's overwhelming the amount of awesome stuff out there. I don't know what to do first! One step at a time I suppose.
